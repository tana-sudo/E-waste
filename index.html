About E-Waste

Electronic waste (e-waste) is any discarded electrical or electronic device. When not handled properly, e-waste releases hazardous substances like lead, mercury, and brominated flame retardants that pollute soil and water and pose health risks to communities — especially informal recyclers.

Key definitions

  • WEEE: Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment.
  • Take-back: Manufacturer or retailer programs for returning old devices.

Safe Data

Always back up and securely erase personal data before recycling devices.

Why It Matters

Health: Exposure to toxins causes respiratory issues and neurological damage. Environment: E-waste contaminates water and soil, harming food systems.

How to Recycle Electronics — Step by Step

  1. Identify: Separate batteries and hazardous parts from the device.
  2. Secure: Back up and wipe personal data using device reset or certified data destruction.
  3. Locate: Use local recycling centers, manufacturer take-back, or certified e-waste events.
  4. Drop-off: Package broken devices safely and deliver them to approved points.
